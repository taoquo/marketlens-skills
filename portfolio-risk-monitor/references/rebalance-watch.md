# Rebalance Watch

Use this file to convert risk diagnosis into research follow-up signals, not personalized allocation instructions. Use `../../references/scoring-standard.md` for shared labels and boundaries.

## Rebalance Watch Signals

Use these as review triggers:

- Single-name or cluster concentration rises because price moved, not because thesis improved.
- Multiple holdings depend on the same catalyst or policy outcome.
- Valuation expands but earnings revisions stop improving.
- A lower-quality name now carries more downside than a higher-quality alternative.
- Currency, rates, or liquidity exposure becomes the dominant portfolio risk.
- A thesis moves from confirmed to delayed, impaired, or broken.
- Evidence gap remains unresolved after the expected disclosure window.

## Candidate Buckets

| Bucket | Meaning | Research Follow-Up |
|---|---|---|
| Add-candidate watch | Attractive if evidence or price improves | Define price/valuation/evidence trigger |
| Hold/watch | Thesis intact but no urgent change | Monitor next data release |
| Trim-review | Risk, valuation, or concentration deserves review | Identify what would reduce risk |
| Exit-review | Thesis break or unacceptable evidence gap | Verify kill condition and alternatives |
| Replace-review | Similar exposure exists with better quality or lower risk | Compare drivers and valuation |
| Avoid | Risk/reward, evidence quality, governance, balance sheet, or structural pressure is not acceptable | Require a new thesis or materially better evidence before reconsidering |

## Compliance Boundary

Do not tell the user exact allocation changes. Say:

- "This name belongs in trim-review because..."
- "This is an add-candidate watch if..."
- "This cluster is the largest shared risk driver..."

Do not say:

- "Buy 5%."
- "Sell half."
- "Move from this stock to that stock."

## Review Cadence

Suggest cadence by risk type:

- Event/catalyst risk: around event date and post-event review window.
- Earnings risk: before results, after guidance, and after revisions settle.
- Macro/liquidity risk: weekly or when regime indicators change.
- Policy risk: after official notices and implementation details.
- Valuation/crowding risk: when price moves faster than revisions.
