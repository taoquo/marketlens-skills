# Priority Framework

Use this file to rank watchlist and portfolio names without giving personalized allocation advice. Apply `../../references/scoring-standard.md` for shared score direction, labels, red flags, and confidence.

## Priority Scorecard

Score each dimension 0 to 3. Use the score to prioritize research attention, not as a mechanical buy/sell signal.

For every dimension, 3 means more attractive or better controlled, and 0 means weak, unavailable, redundant, or high risk.

| Dimension | 3 | 2 | 1 | 0 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Thesis quality | Strong evidence and clear key forces | Plausible but needs monitoring | Thin or narrative-heavy | Unclear |
| Valuation setup | Margin of safety or reset | Reasonable | Rich | Very crowded or unsupported |
| Catalyst / timing | Clear dated catalyst or revision path | Medium-term trigger | Weak timing | No identifiable trigger |
| Risk control | Clear kill conditions and manageable downside | Some defined risks | Risks hard to bound | Unknown or asymmetric downside |
| Portfolio role | Distinct exposure or diversifier | Useful but overlaps | Redundant exposure | Adds same risk without better quality |
| Data confidence | Fresh primary evidence | Mixed but usable | Mostly secondary/stale | Insufficient |

## Research Labels

Use these labels:

- High-priority watch: deserves close monitoring because thesis quality, catalyst, and risk/reward evidence are strong.
- Add-candidate watch: could become attractive if price, valuation, catalyst, or evidence gate improves.
- Hold/watch: thesis remains intact but no clear new action signal.
- Trim-review: valuation, crowding, concentration, or thesis deterioration warrants review.
- Exit-review: kill condition, thesis break, governance issue, or risk concentration warrants review.
- Avoid: risk/reward, evidence quality, governance, balance sheet, or structural pressure is not acceptable.
- Evidence-gap: not enough data for a reliable classification.

Avoid language that tells the user exactly how much to buy, sell, or allocate.

## Ranking Discipline

Separate:

- Best business quality.
- Best valuation setup.
- Best catalyst timing.
- Biggest portfolio risk reducer.
- Most dangerous concentration risk.

Do not collapse these into a single "best stock" unless the user's question is explicitly watchlist priority and evidence supports the ranking.
