# Regional Transmission

Use this file when the user asks about Hong Kong, A-share, China, or cross-market effects.

## US To Global Risk Assets

- Higher real yields raise discount rates and pressure long-duration growth equities.
- USD strength can tighten global financial conditions, especially for markets with FX or external funding pressure.
- Treasury volatility can reduce leverage through risk-parity, volatility-control, and macro-fund channels.
- Easy Fed/Treasury/RRP liquidity can support risk appetite, but crowded positioning can still create drawdown risk.

## Hong Kong

- Hong Kong equities are sensitive to USD liquidity because the HKD is linked to the USD and local rates can follow US funding pressure.
- HIBOR spikes, weak HKD liquidity, or falling HKMA aggregate balance can pressure leveraged and rate-sensitive sectors.
- Southbound flows can offset foreign outflows, but concentration in a few large names can create crowding.
- Internet and growth shares react strongly to US yields, RMB expectations, policy signals, and buyback execution.

## A-Share

- A-shares are more policy-liquidity sensitive than directly Fed-liquidity sensitive, but USD/CNH and northbound flows still affect risk appetite.
- PBOC easing, credit impulse, fiscal policy, ETF support, and sector policy can dominate short-term direction.
- Margin financing, turnover, valuation percentile, and breadth help separate durable risk appetite from short squeeze or policy-driven rebound.
- Northbound flows are useful but should not be treated as the whole market because domestic institutions and retail liquidity often dominate.

## China FX And Rates

- Rising USD/CNH can signal capital outflow pressure, weak growth expectations, or broad USD strength; identify which channel is most supported by evidence.
- Lower China yields can support liquidity but may also reflect weak growth expectations, so pair rate moves with credit and earnings data.
- RMB stability with improving credit or fiscal impulse is more supportive than FX stability alone.

## Cross-Market Conflicts

- If US liquidity is tight but China policy liquidity is easing, separate offshore Hong Kong growth exposure from onshore policy-supported sectors.
- If southbound buying is strong while foreign flows are weak, call out buyer composition and whether it changes valuation risk.
- If A-shares rally while macro data is weak, test whether policy, positioning, or earnings revisions are driving the move.
