# Scenario Framework

Use this file to analyze expectation gaps and event paths.

## Expectation Gap

Before an event, identify:

- What is confirmed: date, agenda, reporting period, legal/regulatory status.
- Consensus expectation: guidance, consensus estimate, published target, official target, or management language.
- Market-implied expectation: price run-up/drawdown, valuation change, implied move, IV rank, short interest, borrow cost, flows, or positioning.
- Variant perception: what a differentiated view expects that is not yet in consensus or market pricing.
- What is uncertain: metric, timing, approval, order size, pricing, margin, adoption, legal exposure.
- What would surprise: upside and downside facts that are not already priced or broadly expected.

Do not call an event bullish only because it is important. A positive event can be negative if expectations are higher.

## Valuation Anchor Impact

Map the event to one or more anchors:

| Anchor | Event Can Change |
|---|---|
| Revenue | TAM, market share, order/backlog, pricing, usage, customer adoption |
| Margin | Mix, utilization, cost, pass-through, scale, reimbursement, pricing power |
| Cash flow | Working capital, capex, collections, buyback capacity, debt service |
| Multiple | Growth durability, risk premium, governance, policy visibility, quality perception |
| Balance sheet | Dilution, refinancing, leverage, solvency, liquidity |
| Legal/regulatory | Market access, liability, compliance cost, operating constraints |

## Scenario Tree

Use three scenarios unless the event is binary:

| Scenario | What Happens | Evidence To Watch | Thesis Read-Through | Price Reaction Path |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Upside | Better than expected in a thesis-relevant way | Specific data that confirms it | Strengthened or upgraded watch | Re-rating, squeeze, or sustained revision if not priced |
| Base | Largely matches expectations | Data that confirms expectations | Neutral or monitor next event | Muted, sell-the-news if crowded, or drift with revisions |
| Downside | Worse than expected or delays confirmation | Specific data that breaks assumption | Delayed, impaired, broken, or exit-review | De-risking, multiple compression, or one-day reset |

For binary regulatory, legal, or approval events, use approved / delayed / rejected or win / settlement / loss as appropriate.

## Probability Discipline

Only assign probabilities when:

- There is enough historical, regulatory, legal, or company-specific evidence.
- The probabilities add to 100%.
- The output explains why the probability is research judgment, not certainty.

If evidence is thin, use qualitative labels: higher likelihood, balanced, lower likelihood, unknown.

## Event Reaction Framework

Use these labels to separate thesis impact from price action:

- Beat and raise: usually strongest if expectations were not already full; watch sustainability and revisions.
- Beat but no raise: can be neutral or sell-the-news if valuation and expectations were elevated.
- Miss but guide up: can strengthen thesis if near-term miss is transitory and forward data improves.
- Sell-the-news: fundamentals meet or beat, but pre-event run-up, high IV, crowded positioning, or no new upside causes pressure.
- Squeeze: upside event meets high short interest, high borrow, low float, or forced de-risking by shorts.
- De-risking: event confirms downside, raises uncertainty, or removes a crowded long narrative.
- No real thesis change: headline moves price but does not affect the core thesis variable.

## Pre-Event Watch Data

Define data before the event:

- Market pricing: stock move, implied move, valuation, short interest, flows.
- Event setup: 1-week, 1-month, and 3-month relative performance, volume change, IV rank, borrow cost, estimate revisions.
- Fundamentals: orders, sales, traffic, usage, pricing, inventory, utilization.
- Management: tone, guidance changes, event agenda, disclosure scope.
- External checks: customer data, regulator docket, industry data, policy timetable.
- Peer read-through: competitor results, supplier/customer commentary, sector revisions.
