# Market Pricing

Use this file to rank catalysts and judge whether the market already prices the event.

## Catalyst Scorecard

Score each dimension from 0 to 3. Use the score to prioritize attention, not as a mechanical trading signal.

| Dimension | 3 | 2 | 1 | 0 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Timing certainty | Confirmed date/time | Confirmed window | Expected but not confirmed | Rumor or unknown |
| Financial impact | Can change core revenue, margin, cash flow, balance sheet, or legal liability | Can change one important metric | Mostly sentiment or second-order impact | Immaterial |
| Expectation gap | Clear variant view versus consensus/market | Some uncertainty remains | Mostly expected | Fully priced or no identifiable gap |
| Market pricing | Setup not fully reflected or risk/reward asymmetric | Partly reflected | Mostly reflected | Crowded, expensive, or no edge |
| Crowding | Low crowding or contrarian setup | Balanced positioning | Crowded but manageable | Extreme crowding or high unwind risk |

Total score:

- 12-15: priority catalyst.
- 8-11: monitor closely.
- 4-7: watchlist only.
- 0-3: likely noise unless new evidence appears.

Do not average away a 0 in timing certainty or financial impact when the request needs a high-conviction catalyst.

## Market Pricing Checklist

For material events, check what the market appears to price:

- Price action: 1-week, 1-month, and 3-month absolute and relative return.
- Volume and liquidity: unusual volume, turnover, float constraints, block trades.
- Valuation: multiple expansion/compression versus peers and history.
- Options: implied move, IV rank/percentile, skew, event premium where available.
- Short pressure: short interest, days to cover, borrow cost, utilization where available.
- Revisions: consensus revenue, EPS, EBITDA, margin, target price, or guidance revisions.
- Flows and positioning: ETF/sector flows, fund positioning, retail flows where relevant and dated.

If options, borrow, or flow data is unavailable, mark it unavailable and lower confidence instead of guessing.

## Interpretation Rules

- Strong fundamentals after a large pre-event run-up may still produce sell-the-news.
- High implied move means the event needs a larger surprise to create favorable price reaction.
- High short interest can create upside convexity only if the event directly challenges the short thesis.
- Low valuation alone is not a catalyst.
- Estimate revisions matter more when the event can start or stop a revision cycle.
- If valuation expands while revisions flatten, treat the setup as fragile.
