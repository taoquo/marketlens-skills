# Value Chain Framework

Use this framework to identify where value accrues across an industry chain.

## Mapping The Chain

Define the value chain in 3-6 segments:

- Upstream inputs: raw materials, IP, equipment, infrastructure, labor, capital, data.
- Midstream production: manufacturing, processing, platform operations, distribution, logistics.
- Downstream demand: customers, channels, end markets, advertisers, consumers, governments.
- Enablers: software, equipment, tooling, financing, compliance, standards, logistics.
- Substitutes and complements: technologies or business models that shift demand or margins.

## Profit Pool Tests

| Test | What To Check |
|---|---|
| Scarcity | Is the scarce resource capacity, IP, brand, data, license, distribution, or balance sheet? |
| Bargaining power | Supplier concentration, customer concentration, switching cost, contract duration |
| Pass-through | Ability to pass input inflation or FX changes into price |
| Operating leverage | Fixed-cost base, utilization sensitivity, incremental margin |
| Capital intensity | Capex cycle, maintenance capex, working capital, replacement cost |
| Regulation | Tariffs, price controls, reimbursement, licensing, antitrust, localization |

## Winner Identification

Prefer companies or segments that have:

- Direct exposure to the bottleneck or profit pool.
- Durable cost advantage, brand, IP, distribution, license, or data scale.
- Balance sheet strength through the downcycle.
- Evidence that industry improvement reaches revenue, margin, or cash flow.
- Valuation that has not already capitalized the full optimistic scenario.

## False Positives

- Revenue exposure without margin capture.
- High volume growth with falling ASP and poor cash conversion.
- Policy support that benefits customers more than producers.
- Upstream cost relief that is competed away downstream.
- Local substitution narrative without verified product adoption.
- Temporary shortage that triggers a large capacity response.

## Read-Through Discipline

When connecting an industry trend to a listed company, state:

- Revenue or profit exposure to the segment.
- Whether exposure is direct, indirect, or narrative-driven.
- Margin transmission path.
- Balance-sheet or execution constraint.
- Valuation caveat and what company-level evidence is still needed.
