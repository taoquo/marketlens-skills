# Research To Trade

Use this file when the user asks how an industry view translates into market expression, timing, catalysts, or risk controls. Keep the output as research guidance, not personalized trading advice. Apply `../../references/scoring-standard.md` for score direction, red flags, and labels.

## Trader View

Summarize the industry view in this structure:

| Item | Read |
|---|---|
| Industry phase | Expansion, recovery, destocking, late-cycle, structural decline, or mixed cycle by segment |
| Trading state | Expectations, valuation sensitivity, earnings revision trend, positioning, and crowding |
| Best expression | Profit pool, bottleneck supplier, cyclical beneficiary, quality leader, basket, hedge, or watchlist |
| Main risk | What would break the market expression: demand, capex, price, margin, policy, valuation, FX, rates, funding |
| Positioning implication | Staged exposure, wait for confirmation, tighten trims, avoid leverage, or watch only |

Avoid generic phrases such as "industry is improving" without naming the traded expression and the failure mode.

## Subsector Scorecard

When an industry has mixed cycles, score each segment from 0 to 3. Use the score as a comparison tool, not a mechanical recommendation.

| Dimension | 3 | 2 | 1 | 0 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Demand strength | Broad and accelerating | Growing but uneven | Stable or narrow | Contracting |
| Supply constraint | Scarce and hard to add | Tight but easing | Balanced | Oversupplied |
| Pricing trend | Rising with pass-through | Stable or mix-positive | Weakening | Falling sharply |
| Profit realization | Revenue converts to margin and cash flow | Partial margin capture | Revenue without clear margin | Poor cash conversion |
| Valuation pressure | Low or already reset | Reasonable | Elevated | Crowded and revision-sensitive |
| Policy risk | Supportive or low | Mixed | Material uncertainty | Active restriction or margin cap |

For risk dimensions, high score means lower pressure or lower risk. Explain any dimension where the score conflicts with the headline thesis.

## Industry-To-Trade Path

Use this path before naming a trade expression:

```text
Industry phase -> profit pool -> best expression -> catalyst window -> stop-error -> portfolio overlap
```

Do not let a favorable industry score replace company-specific valuation, event risk/reward, or portfolio concentration review.

## Company Role Map

Classify listed companies by role before ranking names:

- Core profit pool: best long-term expression when profit capture, cash flow, and competitive durability are verified.
- Bottleneck supplier: preferred expression when scarce assets, IP, capacity, licenses, distribution, or critical inputs drive industry economics.
- Cyclical beneficiary: useful for recovery or inventory restocking phases, but requires exit discipline near peak earnings.
- Theme beta: high market sensitivity to narrative, policy, or risk appetite; suitable only when evidence and valuation support the trade.
- Watchlist only: plausible exposure but insufficient data, poor valuation, weak balance sheet, or unclear execution.

Separate "best business", "best industry exposure", and "best trade expression"; they are often different.

## Catalysts And Stop-Error Conditions

Write catalysts as observable data, not vibes.

Upgrade triggers:

- Demand indicators accelerate or broaden.
- Capacity, inventory, or supply constraints tighten.
- Price, ASP, take rate, or margin trend improves.
- Customer capex/orders/backlog/usage are revised upward.
- Earnings revisions or guidance improve across multiple peers.
- Policy, funding, or regulatory conditions become more supportive.

Cooldown triggers:

- Key customer orders or capex are delayed or revised down.
- Contract price, spot price, ASP, take rate, or margin improvement slows.
- Inventory rebuilds faster than end demand.
- Capacity additions exceed demand.
- Leaders guide below expectations or stop seeing revisions.
- Valuation expands while earnings revisions flatten.

Stop-error / exit-review signals:

- The original bottleneck disappears.
- Profit pool shifts to another segment.
- Revenue growth no longer converts to margin or cash flow.
- Policy changes cap economics or restrict market access.
- Balance-sheet risk rises before the cycle turns.
- Thesis depends mainly on valuation expansion after fundamentals stop improving.

## Research-To-Trade Map

End industry work with this map when the user asks for actionability:

| Bucket | Suitable Areas | Evidence Needed | Main Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Long-term allocation | Durable profit pools, structural leaders, hard-to-replicate bottlenecks | Multi-year demand, cash flow, reinvestment runway, valuation discipline | Overpaying for quality or terminal-growth assumptions |
| Swing trade | Cyclical beneficiaries, inventory recovery, pricing inflection, earnings revision cycle | Near-term data catalysts, revision momentum, valuation reset | Late entry near peak expectations |
| Watch only | Theme beta, early technology, policy narrative, weak balance sheet, unproven substitutes | Commercial validation, margin evidence, balance-sheet support | Narrative drawdown or dilution |
| Avoid / underweight | Structural decline, oversupply, policy margin cap, poor governance, broken cash conversion | Clear reversal catalyst before reconsidering | Value trap |

Name the data releases or company events that can create trading windows. If no dated catalyst exists, state that the view is strategic rather than tactical.
