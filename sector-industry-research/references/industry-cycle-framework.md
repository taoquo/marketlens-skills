# Industry Cycle Framework

Use this framework to classify where an industry sits in its cycle. It is a research heuristic, not a trading system.

## Cycle Stages

| Stage | Evidence Pattern | Research Read-Through |
|---|---|---|
| Early recovery | Demand stabilizes, inventory falls, pricing stops worsening, margins bottom | Watch leaders and balance-sheet survivors |
| Expansion | Demand grows, utilization rises, pricing/mix improves, earnings revisions turn up | Favor quality leaders and operating leverage |
| Late-cycle / crowded | Capacity additions accelerate, margins peak, valuations expand, narratives broaden | Tighten assumptions and monitor supply response |
| Downturn / destocking | Orders weaken, inventory builds, pricing falls, utilization drops | Avoid high leverage and weak pricing power |
| Structural decline | End demand erodes, substitutes gain share, regulation or technology impairs economics | Require deep valuation discount and clear catalyst |

## Core Indicators

- Demand: shipment volume, sales volume, order intake, backlog, usage, traffic, utilization by end customers.
- Pricing: ASP, contract prices, spot prices, take rate, monetization rate, discounting intensity.
- Supply: capacity, production, utilization, capex, new entrants, imports, project pipeline.
- Inventory: channel inventory, days inventory, finished-goods inventory, distributor checks, customer stocking.
- Margin: gross margin, EBITDA margin, spread, unit economics, cost pass-through.
- Balance sheet: working capital, leverage, refinancing, capex commitments.

## Indicator Adjustments By Industry Archetype

- Physical capacity industries: track capacity, utilization, order/backlog, inventory, capex, lead time, and replacement cost.
- Digital/platform industries: use revenue growth, retention, usage, ARPU/take rate, customer acquisition efficiency, regulation, and margin scale rather than physical inventory.
- Consumer/channel industries: track volume versus price/mix, channel inventory, discounting, income sensitivity, penetration, brand strength, and distributor behavior.
- Commodity-like industries: use cost curve, inventory, utilization, spot/contract spread, capacity additions, and marginal producer economics.
- Asset-heavy regulated industries: use occupancy/utilization, regulated returns, tariff/pricing resets, capex cycle, refinancing cost, and policy constraints.
- Science/technology-risk industries: use product validation, adoption milestones, R&D pipeline, approval/qualification status, cash runway, and competitive alternatives.
- Balance-sheet-driven industries: use credit cycle, funding cost, capital adequacy, asset quality, liquidity, leverage, and regulatory capital.

## Cycle Mistakes To Avoid

- Do not call an industry bottom only because valuation is low.
- Do not call strong shipment growth healthy if it is inventory restocking without end demand.
- Do not treat revenue growth as pricing power without checking volume, mix, and cost.
- Do not use one company's management tone as proof of the whole industry cycle.
- Do not average away severe excess capacity or funding stress.
