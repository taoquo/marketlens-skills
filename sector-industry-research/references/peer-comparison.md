# Peer Comparison

Use peer comparison to turn industry conclusions into listed-company read-through without overclaiming stock conclusions.

## Peer Set Construction

- Define geography, listing venue, business segment, customer/end-market exposure, and size threshold.
- Separate pure plays from conglomerates and suppliers/customers.
- Separate global leaders from local substitutes when market structure differs.
- Exclude companies only if the exclusion reason is stated.
- Use local-market peers for valuation when listing venue, liquidity, governance, or policy risk materially changes multiples.

## Comparison Dimensions

| Dimension | Use |
|---|---|
| Exposure | Segment revenue/profit share, customer mix, geography, end market |
| Quality | ROIC/ROE, gross margin, FCF conversion, R&D/product strength |
| Cycle sensitivity | Operating leverage, pricing beta, inventory risk, capex cycle |
| Balance sheet | Net cash/debt, refinancing, working capital, dilution risk |
| Execution | Delivery history, product validation, market share, customer wins |
| Valuation | P/E, EV/EBITDA, EV/sales, P/B, NAV, FCF yield, sector-appropriate metrics |

## Classification

Use these labels as research shorthand, not buy/sell recommendations:

- Core profit pool: captures the largest or highest-quality industry profit pool with verified revenue, margin, and cash-flow linkage.
- Structural leader: high quality, durable share, pricing power, and strong balance sheet.
- Bottleneck supplier: benefits from scarce capacity, IP, equipment, license, infrastructure, data, distribution, or critical materials.
- Cyclical beneficiary: high earnings beta to an improving cycle, but weaker durability.
- Local substitute: benefits from localization or import substitution if adoption is verified.
- High-beta challenger: upside if execution works, but higher balance-sheet or dilution risk.
- Theme beta: mainly expresses narrative, policy, or risk appetite; require higher evidence before treating it as fundamental exposure.
- Value trap: low multiple but weak cycle position, governance, cash flow, or structural demand.
- Watchlist only: exposure is plausible, but data or valuation evidence is insufficient.

## Valuation Context

- Compare valuation to normalized earnings, not only peak or trough earnings.
- Label trailing versus forward multiples and GAAP versus adjusted metrics.
- For cyclicals, avoid calling a low P/E cheap at peak earnings.
- For growth industries, bridge revenue growth to margin and cash flow before using sales multiples.
- For China/Hong Kong sectors, check policy, liquidity, shareholder return, and governance discounts.

## Output Discipline

When ranking companies, separate:

- Industry exposure ranking.
- Business quality ranking.
- Valuation attractiveness.
- Risk level and evidence gaps.
- Trade expression role: core profit pool, bottleneck supplier, cyclical beneficiary, theme beta, or watchlist only.

Do not collapse these into a single "best stock" unless company-level research gates are met.
