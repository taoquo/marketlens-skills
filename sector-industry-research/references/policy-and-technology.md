# Policy And Technology Checks

Use this file when an industry is policy-sensitive, regulated, subsidized, geopolitically exposed, or being reshaped by technology.

## Policy Analysis

Check:

- Official policy objective: growth support, national security, affordability, decarbonization, financial stability, antitrust, consumer protection.
- Mechanism: subsidy, tax credit, procurement, price control, quota, licensing, tariff, export control, capital rule, reimbursement.
- Beneficiary: producers, customers, upstream suppliers, downstream users, incumbents, new entrants, state-owned firms.
- Duration and funding: temporary campaign, multi-year plan, budgeted support, local-government implementation.
- Constraint: compliance cost, margin cap, localization requirement, data/security rule, foreign access restriction.

Do not assume policy support means shareholder returns improve. Identify who captures the subsidy or regulatory benefit.

## Technology Disruption

Check:

- Adoption evidence: shipped products, contracts, users, revenue, utilization, pilots moving to production.
- Cost curve: learning rate, input constraints, capex intensity, unit cost versus incumbent.
- Performance: quality, reliability, latency, yield, safety, regulatory approval, customer switching cost.
- Ecosystem: standards, developer/customer base, distribution, supply chain, service network.
- Incumbent response: price cuts, bundling, capex, M&A, lobbying, product roadmap.

## Localization And Import Substitution

Require evidence beyond narrative:

- Customer qualification or design wins.
- Shipment or revenue contribution.
- Yield/reliability data where relevant.
- Cost and performance gap versus imported alternatives.
- Policy or procurement rule that changes adoption incentives.

## Export Control And Sanctions

State:

- The official rule or list and publication date.
- Covered products, technologies, entities, or end uses.
- Direct exposure and indirect supply-chain exposure.
- Whether alternatives exist and at what cost or performance penalty.
- What disclosure would confirm the company-level impact.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating a pilot as commercial adoption.
- Treating subsidy announcements as profit without checking competition.
- Ignoring that regulation can help incumbents and hurt challengers.
- Confusing technology possibility with revenue timing.
- Treating geopolitical restrictions as uniformly positive for local substitutes.
